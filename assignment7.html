Finally, in activity seven I learned about the components necessary for a home network. Most modern households have a wireless network set up but many of these components are set up by a technician so this is valuable information to know. For starters you need a ISP or internet service provider, there are many options on the market with varying speeds and prices. You will also need a modem which receives from your ISP and then sends it to a Router which creates the wireless network allowing multiple devices to connect. The connection between the devices is made possible by ethernet cables and possible coaxial cable depending on the Modem. This is crucial information and basis to troubleshooting issues in the future.
